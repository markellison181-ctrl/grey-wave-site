## This Week's Numbers

**Ontario Reality Check:**
- New retirement homes licensed in 2025: **9 properties** (847 total suites)
- Ontario's 75+ population growth: **31,000 people**
- Math: **27 seniors per new suite added**

No wonder waitlists are 18-24 months. And this is just retirement residence — assisted living and memory care have even worse supply gaps.

**Meanwhile in BC:**
- Average retirement suite cost: **$8,200/month**
- Provincial vacancy rate: **2.3%**
- Pipeline per 1,000 seniors: **3.2 units** (vs Ontario: 5.8 units)

The most expensive province has the weakest supply pipeline. That's not economics — that's regulatory dysfunction.

## The Transition Shock

Here's what nobody talks about: the same families fighting over $1.2M townhouses in Mississauga will face sticker shock when aging parents need care.

$6-8K/month for retirement residence. $9-12K/month for assisted living. $15K+ for memory care.

The housing crisis isn't just about buying your first home. It ripples through every lifecycle decision.

## Policy Watch

**CMHC Changes Coming:**
The National Housing Strategy refresh is dropping March 15th. Early signals suggest seniors housing will get dedicated funding streams for the first time since 2019.

**Ontario RHRA Update:**
New transparency requirements take effect April 1st. Every licensed home must publish wait times, base rates, and care levels online. Good for families. Terrifying for operators with bad metrics.

## Deep Dive: The 89,000 Unit Gap

Our analysis of Ontario's housing demand shows an 89,000-unit shortage across all seniors housing categories by 2031. But the breakdown matters:

- **Independent living**: 34,000 units (feasible with current models)
- **Assisted living**: 31,000 units (needs policy support)  
- **Memory care**: 24,000 units (requires complete rethink)

The independent living gap is solvable with capital. The memory care gap requires innovation we haven't seen yet.
